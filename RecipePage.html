Curry

Curry is a rich and aromatic dish made with a blend of spices, herbs, and ingredients like meat, vegetables, or legumes. Originating from South Asian cuisine, it has evolved into various regional styles, including Indian, Thai, and Japanese curries. Typically prepared with a sauce or gravy, it can be mild, spicy, or creamy, depending on the spices and base ingredients used.

prep time

30 min

cook time

15 min

servings

6 servings

instructions

step 1

Heat Oil & Sauté Aromatics – Heat oil or butter in a pan and sauté chopped onions, garlic, and ginger until golden brown.

step 2

Add Spices – Stir in spices like turmeric, cumin, coriander, garam masala, and chili powder to release their flavors.

step 3

Cook the Protein or Vegetables – Add meat, tofu, or vegetables and cook until slightly browned.

step 4

Add Tomatoes or Paste – Mix in chopped tomatoes or tomato paste for depth and richness. Cook until they break down.

step 5

Pour in Liquid – Add water, coconut milk, or broth to create the curry sauce. Let it simmer.

step 6

Season & Simmer – Add salt, pepper, and other seasonings. Let everything cook together until flavors meld.

step 7

Garnish & Serve – Finish with fresh herbs (like cilantro) and serve with rice or bread. Enjoy!

ingredients

Oil (2 tbsp)

Onion (1 large)

Garlic (3 cloves)

Ginger (1-inch)

Turmeric (1 tsp)

Garam Masala (1 tsp)

chicken (500g)

tools

Pan or pot

Knife

Cutting board

Stirring spoon

Measuring spoons

Grater

Bowl

Taco

A taco is a popular Mexican dish made with a soft or crispy tortilla filled with a variety of ingredients, such as seasoned meat, beans, cheese, and vegetables. It is often topped with fresh garnishes like cilantro, onions, salsa, and lime for extra flavor. Tacos come in many styles, including street tacos, fish tacos, and barbacoa, each with unique regional flavors. They can be enjoyed as a quick snack or a full meal, making them a versatile and beloved dish worldwide.

prep time

40 min

cook time

20 min

servings

8 servings

instructions

step 1

Prepare the Filling – Cook your choice of meat (beef, chicken, pork, or fish) with seasoning, or use beans for a vegetarian option.

step 2

Warm the Tortillas – Heat corn or flour tortillas on a pan, grill, or directly over a flame for a slight char.

step 3

Prepare Toppings – Chop onions, cilantro, lettuce, tomatoes, or other fresh ingredients.

step 4

Make the Sauce – Prepare salsa, guacamole, or a creamy sauce for extra flavor.

step 5

Assemble the Tacos – Place the cooked filling onto the tortillas and add toppings.

step 6

Garnish & Season – Squeeze fresh lime, sprinkle cheese, and add a final touch of seasoning.

step 7

Serve & Enjoy – Serve warm and enjoy with sides like rice, beans, or chips!

ingredients

Tortillas – 8 small corn or flour tortillas

Protein – 500g (1 lb) cooked beef, chicken, pork, fish, or beans

Onion – 1 small onion, finely chopped

Cilantro – ¼ cup, chopped

Lime – 2 limes, cut into wedges

Cheese – ½ cup, crumbled or shredded

Salsa – ½ cup (red or green)

tools

Pan or grill

Knife

Cutting board

Tongs

Mixing bowl

Spoon

Plate

Cheesecake

Cheesecake is a rich and creamy dessert made with a smooth cheese-based filling, typically cream cheese, eggs, and sugar, on a crumbly crust, often made of crushed cookies or graham crackers. It can be baked or no-bake and comes in various flavors, topped with fruits, sauces, or whipped cream.

prep time

10 min

cook time

60 min

servings

12 servings

instructions

step 1

Prepare the Crust – Crush cookies or graham crackers and mix with melted butter.

step 2

Press the Crust – Firmly press the mixture into a pan and chill or bake it.

step 3

Make the Filling – Beat cream cheese, sugar, eggs, and vanilla until smooth.

step 4

Pour the Filling – Spread the mixture evenly over the crust.

step 5

Bake or Chill – Bake in the oven or refrigerate for a no-bake version.

step 6

Cool Completely – Let it cool at room temperature, then chill in the fridge.

step 7

Add Toppings & Serve – Garnish with fruit, sauce, or whipped cream before serving.

ingredients

Cream cheese – 500g (2 cups)

Sugar – ¾ cup

Eggs – 3 large

Vanilla extract – 1 tsp

Graham crackers (or biscuits) – 1½ cups, crushed

Butter – ½ cup, melted

Sour cream (or heavy cream) – ½ cup

tools

Springform pan

Mixer (hand or stand)

Bowl

Measuring cups and spoons

Spatula

Oven

Cooling rack

Pizza

Pizza is a popular Italian dish consisting of a round, flat dough base topped with tomato sauce, cheese, and various toppings like meats, vegetables, and herbs. It is typically baked in an oven until the crust is crispy and the cheese is melted and bubbly. There are many variations, from classic Margherita to creative, gourmet options.

prep time

15 min

cook time

45 min

servings

14 servings

instructions

step 1

Prepare the Dough – Mix flour, water, yeast, salt, and oil to form a dough, then let it rise.

step 2

Preheat the Oven – Heat the oven to 475°F (245°C).

step 3

Shape the Dough – Roll or stretch the dough into a round shape on a floured surface.

step 4

Add Sauce – Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce over the dough.

step 5

Add Toppings – Layer cheese, meats, vegetables, and any other toppings.

step 6

Bake the Pizza – Place the pizza in the oven and bake for 10-15 minutes until the crust is golden and the cheese is melted.

step 7

Slice & Serve – Let it cool for a couple of minutes, then slice and serve.

ingredients

Pizza dough – 1 ball (about 250g)

Tomato sauce – ½ cup

Mozzarella cheese – 1½ cups, shredded

Pepperoni (or other meats) – ¼ cup, sliced

Bell peppers – ¼ cup, thinly sliced

Olive oil – 1 tbsp (for brushing the crust)

Dried oregano – 1 tsp (for seasoning)

tools

Oven

Pizza stone or baking sheet

Rolling pin

Pizza cutter or knife

Mixing bowl

Spatula

Measuring cups and spoons

Sushi

Sushi is a traditional Japanese dish made with vinegared rice, often paired with raw or cooked seafood, vegetables, and seaweed. It comes in various forms, such as rolls (maki), nigiri (hand-pressed rice with toppings), and sashimi (sliced raw fish without rice). Sushi is typically served with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger.

prep time

5 min

cook time

30 min

servings

5 servings

instructions

step 1

Cook the Rice – Rinse and cook sushi rice, then mix with rice vinegar, sugar, and salt.

step 2

Prepare the Fillings – Slice fish, vegetables, or other ingredients into thin strips.

step 3

Lay Out the Nori – Place a sheet of seaweed (nori) on a bamboo rolling mat.

step 4

Spread the Rice – Evenly spread a thin layer of sushi rice over the nori.

step 5

Add Fillings – Place fish, vegetables, or other fillings in a line near the edge.

step 6

Roll the Sushi – Use the bamboo mat to tightly roll the sushi into a log.

step 7

Slice & Serve – Cut the roll into bite-sized pieces and serve with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger.

ingredients

Sushi rice – 2 cups (cooked)

Rice vinegar – ¼ cup

Nori (seaweed sheets) – 4 sheets

Fresh fish (salmon/tuna) or imitation crab – 200g (sliced)

Cucumber – ½, thinly sliced

Avocado – 1, sliced

Soy sauce – For dipping

tools

Rice cooker or pot – For cooking sushi rice

Bamboo rolling mat (makisu) – For rolling sushi

Sharp knife – For slicing sushi rolls

Cutting board – For preparing ingredients

Mixing bowl – For seasoning the rice

Measuring cups and spoons – For accurate ingredient portions

Plastic wrap – To prevent sticking while rolling
